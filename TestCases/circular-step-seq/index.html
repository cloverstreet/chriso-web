CIRCULAR STEP SEQUENCER v0.1

Circular Step Sequencer

Concentric circular tracks, each with its own step count. A single clock hand sweeps around at the master tempo; whenever it passes a lit cell on any track, that track triggers its synth voice at the cell's intensity (0 = silent, 9 = full).

Linear step-sequencers force all tracks to share a common length. Circular tracks with independent step counts can drift in and out of phase forever — same idea as a tanpura's coprime divisors but editable per-step.

How to play

  • Click START. The hand begins sweeping.
  • Click a cell in any track row to set its strength. Click the same cell again to step 0 → 1 → 2 … 9 → 0.
  • Change a track's STEPS to re-grid it (cells preserved where possible).
  • Each track has its own PITCH (typeable, accepts Hz or a note name like A3, C#5). The MUTE button silences a track without losing its pattern.
  • TEMPO controls the master sweep rate (BPM at 4 steps per beat for the shortest track — actual per-track step time scales with each track's step count).

Presets

  • 6 preset slots — click to recall, shift+click to save.
  • Saves all tracks: step counts, cells, pitches, mutes, tempo.
  • Stored in localStorage.

About this build (v0.1)

  • 4 tracks (8 / 11 / 13 / 16 steps default). The full 8-track version is the next iteration.
  • Sound is a simple internal sine-osc with short envelope per step trigger. The architecture is ready to swap that synth for WebMIDI out or a richer voice (e.g. DronorParty's KS strings) once the sequencer itself is solid.
  • Per-track step count and pitch are editable; cell strength edits work; presets save/load. Drag-to-paint, swing, accents- per-bar, MIDI out — future versions.
