How to use
1. Open this page in two tabs. Each gets a unique peer ID via the public PeerJS broker.
2. In both tabs: Grant Mic Access → pick device → Open Mic.
3. In tab A: copy your peer ID. Switch to tab B: paste into Remote peer ID, click Connect. Tab A auto-answers.
4. Audio flows both ways, peer-to-peer (broker is only used for handshake — actual audio is direct).
5. Drag the gain thumb on the OUT meter (your mic → peer) and IN meter (peer → your ears) to adjust levels.
6. Stats panel shows RTT, jitter, codec, ICE candidate types — useful for diagnosing whether the connection is direct (host/srflx) or relayed (relay).

What you're proving: signaling works, two-way audio works peer-to-peer, per-peer gain control on the receive side. The room (Sandbox/tellofoam) generalizes this to N peers.
