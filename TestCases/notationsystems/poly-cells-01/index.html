Polymetric Looping Cells

Pitch mapping. Choose a tuning system:

Notation test case 02 — three cells of different lengths looping on one shared tempo clock, so they phase against each other. Pitch comes from a selectable tuning system (see Pitch mapping); default is 12-tone equal temperament, exact-Hz via Web Audio.

Offsets (per line): shift rotates the notation left/right by the chosen unit (1/16…1/2) — a note crossing the loop end splits into tied pieces; phase slides the playhead −100…+100% of a quarter without renotating.
Edit the notes: click a note to select it; click again to toggle note↔rest. Drag up/down — or ↑/↓ — to change pitch (by scale degree); ←/→ reorders it. Number keys: 2=16th, 3=8th, 4=quarter, 5=half, 6=whole, 0=rest. Delete removes. Randomize re-rolls pitches (same lengths); Undo reverts it.
Presets: shift-click a numbered box to save the current notes + offsets (not the tuning); click to recall. Save/recall a whole set of 10 as a named bank (banks do store the tuning). A built-in factory ET bank ramps from simple (1) to complex (10).
