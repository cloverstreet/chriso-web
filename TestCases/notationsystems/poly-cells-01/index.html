Polymetric Looping Cells

Pitch mapping — . The cells are notated at their nearest quarter-tone (Stein-Zimmermann accidentals); the small number is the pélog degree and the pitch you hear is exact. Cents = how far the true pélog pitch sits above (+)/below (−) the notated note.

Notation test case 02 — three cells of different lengths looping on one shared tempo clock, so they phase against each other. Pitch is a 7-tone pélog at exact frequencies (microtonal, Web Audio).

Offsets (per line): shift rotates the notation left/right by the chosen unit (1/16…1/2) — a note crossing the loop end splits into tied pieces; phase slides the playhead −100…+100% of a quarter without renotating.
Edit the notes: click a note to select it; click it again to toggle note↔rest. Drag up/down — or ↑/↓ — to change pitch (by pélog degree); ←/→ reorders it. Number keys set its value: 2=16th, 3=8th, 4=quarter, 5=half, 6=whole, 0=rest. Delete removes it. The cell's length updates automatically (shown in its header).
Presets: shift-click a numbered box to save the current notes + offsets (not tempo/gain); click to recall. Save/recall a whole set of 10 as a named bank.
