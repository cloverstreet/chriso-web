Polymetric Looping Cells

notation test case 02 — three cells of different length and meter looping on one shared tempo clock, so they phase against each other and realign only after their common multiple. Pitch is a 7-tone pélog played at exact frequencies (microtonal, via Web Audio). Independent per-cell tempo is the planned next step.

Cells: A = 2 bars of 4/4 (8 beats) · B = 1 bar of 5/4 (5 beats) · C = 1 bar of 3/4 (3 beats). Common multiple LCM(8,5,3) = 120 beats before all three realign at their starts.
Tuning — . Staff position on the cells (left) is an indicative scaffold only (the number is the scale degree; the pitch you hear is exact). The table here gives each degree's approximate Western notation using Stein-Zimmermann quarter-tone accidentals — the mini-staff is rounded to the nearest quarter-tone, and the cents column is how far the true pélog pitch sits above (+) or below (−) that notated note. Swap the cents array in lib/tunings.js to try another gamelan.
