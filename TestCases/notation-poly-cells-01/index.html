Polymetric Looping Cells

notation test case 02 — three cells of different length and meter looping on one shared tempo clock, so they phase against each other. Pitch is a 7-tone pélog played at exact frequencies (microtonal, via Web Audio).

Cells: A = 2 bars of 4/4 (8 beats) · B = 1 bar of 5/4 (5 beats) · C = 1 bar of 3/4 (3 beats). Beats line up vertically across the three. Common multiple LCM(8,5,3) = 120 beats before they realign.
Offsets (per line): ⅛ shifts the notation by an eighth-note (a quarter crossing the loop end splits into two tied eighths); phase slides the playhead 0–300% of a quarter without renotating.
Tuning — . Staff position on the cells (left) is an indicative scaffold only (the number is the scale degree; the pitch you hear is exact).
