How to use
Monitor mode — headphones on, hear yourself. Drag the gain thumb on the OUT meter to set monitor level. The signal bypasses every WebRTC/network stage, so the latency you hear is your local audio I/O budget alone (≈ baseLatency + outputLatency).

Probe mode — speakers on, headphones off. Click Run Probe. A 4 kHz click fires; the worklet captures the mic for 0.5 s and finds the click in the returned audio. Round-trip = (click index ÷ sample rate). Subtract acoustic propagation (3.4 ms / m × distance) to isolate the electrical path through your machine.

What "electrical" tells you: the floor your hardware adds — driver buffers, ADC, DAC, OS scheduling. This is the irreducible piece tellofoam has to add network latency on top of. Tighter floor = better madrigal odds.

Fireface at small buffer sizes (32-64 samples) should land electrical near 5-15 ms. Built-in macbook mic at default buffer often hits 30-50 ms.
